How to Get to Budapest Airport

Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD) is located 16 km southeast of the city center. Well-signposted from all major roads.

🚗

From City Center

Take Üllői út or M3 motorway towards the airport. Follow signs to "Liszt Ferenc Airport". 25-35 min drive.

🛣️

From M0 Ring Road

Exit at junction 57 onto Route 4 (Ferihegyi út). Off-site parking located along Route 4.

📍

GPS Coordinates

Terminal 2: 47.4369° N, 19.2556° E

⏰

Recommended Arrival

Terminal: 2h before Off-site: 2.5-3h before (for shuttle)

⚠️

Traffic Tips

Avoid rush hours (7-9 AM, 4-7 PM). Use M0 ring road to bypass city traffic.

Getting Around

Transport To & From the City

Options for traveling between Budapest Airport and the city center.

🚕

Taxi

Official airport taxi service available at Terminal 2. Fixed zone-based pricing to city center.

💰 ~9,000-12,000 Ft to center ⏱️ 25-40 min ✓ 24/7 available

💡 Use only official Főtaxi from the taxi stand. Avoid touts inside terminal.

🚐

Airport Shuttle (miniBUD)

Shared door-to-door shuttle service. Book online in advance for best rates.

💰 ~3,500-5,500 Ft/person ⏱️ 40-60 min (with stops) ✓ Door-to-door service

💡 Book return journey together for discount. Available 24/7.

🚌

Bus 100E - Direct Airport Bus

Direct express bus to Deák Ferenc tér (city center). No stops in between. Fastest public transport option.

💰 2,200 Ft (special ticket) ⏱️ 35-40 min 🕐 Every 20-30 min

💡 Runs 5:00-1:20. Buy ticket at airport BKK machines or online. Regular passes NOT valid!

🚌

Bus 200E + Metro M3

Budget option using regular public transport. Take 200E to Kőbánya-Kispest, then transfer to M3 metro to city center.

💰 450 Ft × 2 = 900 Ft ⏱️ 50-60 min total 🎫 2 tickets needed

💡 Cheapest option! You need 2 separate tickets: one for bus 200E, one for metro M3. Validate each ticket when boarding. Or buy a 24h travel card (2,500 Ft) for unlimited rides.

Pro Tips

Essential Tips for Drivers

Make your airport experience smooth with these practical tips.

1

Arrive Early

For terminal parking, arrive 2 hours before. For off-site with shuttle, add 30-45 extra minutes for transfer time.

2

Book in Advance

Official airport parking can be booked at least 6 hours ahead. Pre-booking often secures better rates.

3

Fuel Up Before

Fill your tank before heading to the airport. This way your car is ready when you return from your trip.

4

Save Your Spot

Take a photo of your parking spot number and row. Large car parks can be confusing after a long trip.

5

Check for Discounts

Many off-site operators offer early booking discounts and loyalty programs. Check their websites for deals.

6

Know the Shuttle

Off-site shuttles usually run 24/7 and track your flight. Call when you land pickup within 5-10 minutes.
